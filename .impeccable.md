## Design Context

### Users

The primary visitors arrive from Ahmed's TikTok or Instagram content. They are curious builders, founders, creators, and Egyptian retail investors who either want the exact practical resource mentioned in a video or want to understand the person and projects behind The Barbarian Project. Their two main jobs are: get a useful prompt and set it up quickly, or browse Ahmed's work without being forced through a funnel.

### Brand Personality

Curious, candid, and resourceful. The voice is direct and conversational, with a Cairo-based building-in-public energy: ambitious without pretending to be a guru, specific about what worked, and honest about limits, uncertainty, and mistakes. Visitors should feel intrigued, respected, and equipped to try something themselves.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the existing light, iridescent liquid-glass identity: soft cool neutrals, vivid indigo and lime accents, expressive Bricolage Grotesque headings, editorial Instrument Serif moments, and restrained spatial motion. New resource experiences should feel like opening a useful field note inside the same personal world, not entering a generic SaaS landing page. Maintain the light theme and the existing accessible reduced-motion behavior.

### Design Principles

1. Give visitors a choice immediately: resource seekers can go straight to the prompt, while everyone else can continue into the portfolio without friction.
2. Make practical value obvious and copyable: one primary action, short setup steps, strong feedback, and no email gate.
3. Preserve editorial honesty: explain that the monitor is a research workflow, not financial advice or a prediction engine, and never imply guaranteed returns.
4. Use progressive disclosure: summarize the workflow first and reveal the long prompt only when the visitor wants the full detail.
5. Design mobile-first for social traffic, with keyboard-accessible controls, clear focus states, comfortable touch targets, and reduced-motion support.
